Chiska’s Sausage Stuffing

16 oz. bread cut into 1 inch pieces (2 loaves of 2 day old baguettes, if possible)
1 lb. sage pork sausage, casings removed
1/3 cup butter
1 ½ cups finely chopped yellow or brown onion
1 cup chopped celery (2-3 stalks)
5 large cloves garlic, minced (or 1 ½ TBS dried minced garlic)
½ cup chopped flat–leafed parsley (optional)
1 ½ TBS. fresh chopped sage
1 TBS fresh chopped rosemary, or thyme, or both
¾ tsp sea salt (or more to taste)
½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
2 cans low sodium chicken stock
2 eggs, lightly whisked
¾ cup cranberries (optional)

Preheat oven to 350*. Place 1” bread cubes into a large bowl and set aside. In a large sauté pan, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Add to the bread cubes bowl. In the empty sauté pan, melt the butter and add the onions, celery, garlic, parsley, sage, rosemary, thyme, salt and pepper. Sauté it over medium heat for 10 minutes, until the vegetables are softened. Take off the heat and mix in the chicken stock. Add the eggs and cranberries. Pour onto the bread and sausage, mixing really well with a wooden spoon until all the liquid has been absorbed. Pour it all into a 9x13 pan, sprayed with olive oil. Bake for 30 minutes, until browned on the top and hot in the middle. Serve warm.

Note:

If you don’t have day old bread, use this technique to dry it out: Place the bread cubes in a single layer on a sheet pan and bake at 350* for 7-10 minutes, or until dried and toasted.
