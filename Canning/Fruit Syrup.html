Syrup for Canning Fruit

When canning fruit (apricots, pears, or peaches) you peel and slice the fruit and place it directly into clean, hot bottles (pack them tightly.) After the bottle is completely full of fruit, add some syrup then place on the lids and rings and boil them (“process”) them in a canner for 20 minutes. (See Preparation for Canning above)

To make syrup, place sugar and water into a saucepan over medium heat and cook until the sugar is dissolved.

3 cups sugar to 1-quart water= Heavy syrup (use for canning Apricots)

2 cups sugar to 1-quart water= Medium syrup (use for canning peaches and pears)

1 cup sugar to 1-quart water= light syrup (we never use this)

NOTE: 1 full 5-gallon bucket makes about 14 quarts.
