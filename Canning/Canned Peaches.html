How to Can Peaches

Fill the canner with hot water about 2/3rds full.

Place canner on stove top and turn on high, until boiling.

Put quart bottles and bottle rings into the dishwasher and run it- without soap. (The bottles need to be cleaned first. This warms the bottles up, so they won’t crack when you put them into the boiling water of the canner to seal the lids.)

Mix sugar and water together for syrup. Put it into a pot and heat up. (The ratio of sugar to water is 2 cups sugar to 1-quart water.)

Boil canning lids in pot of water for 10 minutes (to kill all the germs.)

Peel and cut peaches into half.

Pack them tightly into cleaned quart bottles (pit side down).

Pour hot sugar syrup into the bottles filled with fruit. Pour up to the bottom of the neck of the bottle.

Place lids and rings on tight.

Put bottles into the canner and boil them for 20 minutes. (That is called “processing”).

Take them out and let them cool on a towel on the countertop.

Make sure all the lids are suctioned on tight (“sealed”) before storing them.
