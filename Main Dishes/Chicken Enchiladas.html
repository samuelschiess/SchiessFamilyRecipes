Chicken Enchiladas- a mixture of Beth Andersen and Jamila William’s recipes

2-3 lbs. chicken tenders or breasts- cooked and cubed (can use leftovers or canned)
½ cup salsa
2 cans cream of chicken soup
1 can olives- chopped
8 oz. sour cream
1-2 cups cheese- grated
8-10 medium flour or corn tortilla shells

In a frying pan, fry chicken with 1TBS oil on medium heat. Season with salt and pepper to taste. When the chicken is no longer pink in the middle, take it off the heat and let cool, then cube. Set it aside. In a pot, heat salsa, chicken soup, olives, sour cream and 1 cup of cheese until cheese is melted. Set aside ½ of this mixture to pour on top of the enchiladas. Add chicken to the remaining sauce. Put the chicken and sauce mixture into a tortilla shell, fold over twice, and place it into a 9x13 pan. Continue this step until the pan is full of filled tortilla shells. Pour the sauce without the chicken on top of the shells and top with the remaining cheese. Bake at 350* for 20-25 minutes or until the sauce is bubbly.
